A note on the painting's name

The Philadelphia Museum of Art catalogues this work as Bunga Dyo, the Legend of Red Avalokiteshvara. This project calls the deity Bunga Dyo, the name by which his devotees know him: the god of Bunga, the village of Bungamati where he resides for part of each year. He is also Karunamaya, the Compassionate One; Rato Matsyendranath; and the Red Avalokiteshvara. The name chosen here reflects what the painting is in its own context: not a museum artifact, but the image of a living object of devotion in Newar Buddhism.
